About Valorant

Valorant is a free-to-play first-person shooter developed and published by Riot Games. The game was officially released on June 2, 2020, and has quickly become a popular choice in the competitive gaming scene.

Characters

Valorant features a diverse roster of agents, each with unique abilities. Here is the complete list of agents:

  • Jett - Agile and aggressive with abilities to enhance mobility and execute high-risk plays.
    • Cloudburst: Deploys a brief vision-blocking cloud.
    • Updraft: Allows Jett to jump higher for better positioning.
    • Tailwind: Dashes in the direction of her choice, useful for quick escapes or repositioning.
    • Blade Storm: Equips Jett with a set of throwing knives that deal critical damage on headshots.
  • Phoenix - Fire-based duelist with self-healing abilities and high mobility.
    • Blaze: Creates a wall of fire that damages enemies and heals Phoenix if he stands in it.
    • Curveball: Throws a flashbang that curves around corners.
    • Hot Hands: Throws a fireball that damages enemies and heals Phoenix.
    • Run It Back: Marks Phoenix’s location. If he dies or activates this ability again, he respawns at the marked location.
  • Yoru - A stealthy duelist who excels at deception and misdirection.
    • Fakeout: Deploys a decoy that mimics Yoru's footsteps.
    • Blindside: Throws a flashbang that blinds enemies.
    • Gatecrash: Teleports Yoru to a marked location.
    • Dimensional Drift: Enter a parallel dimension, making him invisible and invulnerable.
  • Neon - Fast-paced duelist with high mobility and electric abilities.
    • High Voltage: Deploys a burst of electricity to damage and slow enemies.
    • Relay Bolt: Throws a bolt that explodes in a chain lightning effect.
    • Speed Boost: Increases movement speed significantly for a short duration.
    • Overdrive: Activates a high-speed dash that deals damage to enemies on contact.
  • Raze - Explosive duelist with abilities designed for area control and high damage.
    • Blast Pack: Throws a satchel charge that deals damage on explosion and can be used to reposition.
    • Paint Shells: Throws a cluster grenade that deals damage over a large area.
    • Boom Bot: Deploys a robot that seeks out enemies and explodes on contact.
    • Showstopper: Equips Raze with a powerful rocket launcher that deals massive area damage.
  • Chamber - A duelist with a focus on precision and tactical superiority.
    • Trademark: Deploys a trap that slows and reveals enemies.
    • Headhunter: Equips a pistol with precision aiming abilities.
    • Rendezvous: Teleports Chamber to a marked location.
    • Tour de Force: Equips a powerful sniper rifle that kills enemies with a single shot and creates a zone that slows down enemies.
  • Sova - Skilled at gathering information and disrupting enemy strategies.
    • Owl Drone: Deploys a drone that can scout and mark enemies.
    • Shock Bolt: Fires an arrow that explodes into a burst of electric damage.
    • Recon Bolt: Deploys an arrow that reveals enemies in its line of sight.
    • Hunter’s Fury: Fires a long-range energy beam that deals damage and reveals enemies.
  • Breach - Uses area-of-effect abilities to disrupt and incapacitate enemies.
    • Aftershock: Deploys an explosive charge through walls that damages and disrupts enemies.
    • Flashpoint: Throws a charge that blinds enemies in its line of sight.
    • Fault Line: Sends out a seismic blast that disorients and slows enemies.
    • Rolling Thunder: Sends out a shockwave that knocks up and disorients enemies.
  • Skye - A versatile initiator with abilities focused on healing and area control.
    • Regrowth: Heals allies over time within a certain range.
    • Trailblazer: Deploys a creature that can be controlled to flash and disorient enemies.
    • Guiding Light: Throws a bird that can be transformed into a flashbang.
    • Seekers: Sends out seekers that track and reveal enemies.
  • KAY/O - Provides utility and disruption with a focus on combat support.
    • FRAG/ment: Throws a grenade that fragments into multiple smaller grenades.
    • FLASH/drive: Throws a flashbang that can be used to blind enemies.
    • FRAG/drive: Throws a grenade that can be used to cause damage over time.
    • NULL/cmd: Sends out an EMP burst that disables enemy abilities and deals damage over time.
    • Sage - Focused on healing and area denial.
      • Barrier Orb: Deploys a large wall that blocks vision and can be used to fortify positions.
      • Slow Orb: Deploys a sphere that slows down enemies who walk through it.
      • Healing Orb: Heals allies over time within its radius.
      • Resurrection: Revives a fallen ally with full health.
    • Killjoy - Uses gadgets to control and secure areas.
      • Nanoswarm: Deploys a grenade that explodes into a swarm of nanobots dealing damage.
      • Alarmbot: Deploys a bot that detects and explodes when enemies are nearby.
      • Turret: Deploys a turret that shoots at enemies in its line of sight.
      • Lockdown: Deploys a device that emits a pulse, slowing and disorienting enemies within its range.
    • Cypher - Focuses on gathering intelligence and controlling enemy movement.
      • Trapwire: Deploys a wire that slows and damages enemies who trigger it.
      • Cyber Cage: Deploys a cage that blocks vision and slows enemies who enter it.
      • Spycam: Deploys a camera that can be remotely controlled to scout and reveal enemies.
      • Neural Theft: Reveals the location of all remaining enemies by extracting information from a dead enemy's body.
    • Fade - Utilizes fear and disorientation to control the battlefield.
      • Haunt: Deploys a ghost that reveals and disorients enemies.
      • Seize: Throws a sphere that slows and damages enemies.
      • Fright: Creates a field that causes fear and hinders enemy movement.
      • Reckoning: Reveals and terrorizes enemies within a large area.
    • Omen - Utilizes smoke and teleportation to manipulate the battlefield.
      • Shrouded Step: Teleports Omen to a nearby location.
      • Dark Cover: Deploys a smoke screen that obscures vision and can be adjusted in size and duration.
      • Paranoia: Sends out a shadowy projectile that blinds and damages enemies in its path.
      • Ultimate: Reveals enemy locations on the map for a short duration.
    • Viper - Uses poison and smoke to control and choke points.
      • Snake Bite: Deploys a poison cloud that damages and debilitates enemies.
      • Cloudburst: Creates a cloud of smoke that obscures vision and slows down enemies.
      • Poison Cloud: Creates a toxic screen that damages and reveals enemies.
      • Viper’s Pit: Creates a large cloud of poison that obscures vision and damages enemies.
    • Astra - Manipulates the battlefield with stars that can be activated for various effects.
      • Stars: Deploys stars that can be activated for smokes, gravity wells, and more.
      • Nova Pulse: Creates a gravity well that pulls enemies toward the center and disorients them.
      • Gravity Well: Deploys a star that creates a vortex, slowing and pulling enemies.
      • Astral Form: Allows Astra to reposition and view the battlefield from an overhead perspective.
    • Harbor - Controls the battlefield with water-based abilities.
      • Glimpse: Creates a water barrier that blocks vision and slows enemies.
      • Flood: Sends out a wave of water that damages and slows enemies.
      • Current: Deploys a tidal wave that pushes enemies back and deals damage.
      • Deluge: Creates a large area of water that obscures vision and slows enemies.
    • Iso - Uses ice-based abilities to control the battlefield.
      • Frostbite: Creates an area of ice that slows and damages enemies.
      • Ice Wall: Deploys a large ice wall that blocks vision and movement.
      • Glacier: Creates an ice dome that provides cover and slows enemies.
      • Arctic Storm: Unleashes a storm of ice shards that deal damage and slow enemies.
    • Clove - Uses plant-based abilities to control and obstruct the battlefield.
      • Vine Trap: Deploys a trap that ensnares and damages enemies.
      • Bloom: Creates a zone of growth that heals allies and damages enemies.
      • Thorn: Sends out thorns that deal damage and slow enemies.
      • Nature's Wrath: Creates a large area of vegetation that slows and damages enemies.

    Agent Roles

    Agents in Valorant are categorized into four main roles, each fulfilling different strategic purposes in the game:

    • Duelists: Aggressive agents designed to excel in combat and lead the charge. Examples: Jett, Phoenix.
    • Initiators: Agents that create opportunities for their team by disrupting the enemy or gathering information. Examples: Sova, Breach.
    • Sentinels: Defensive agents who provide support and protection for their team, often excelling in holding positions and securing areas. Examples: Sage, Killjoy.
    • Controllers: Agents that manipulate the battlefield with abilities that control vision and space, often used to execute strategies and manage enemy movement. Examples: Omen, Viper.

    Maps

    Valorant features several maps, each with unique layouts and strategies. Here are some of the main maps:

    • Binds: A map with teleportation portals and tight corners.
    • Haven: Features three bomb sites and an open layout.
    • Split: Known for its verticality and tight chokepoints.
    • Ascent: A map with a central open area and multiple sightlines.
    • Fracture: Introduces a unique central split and complex terrain.

    Strategy Guides

    Effective strategies can turn the tide of a game. Here are some key strategies:

    • Communication: Coordinate with your team to execute plans and share information.
    • Site Control: Take control of key sites and use your abilities to gain an advantage.
    • Map Knowledge: Learn the layout of each map to effectively plan your moves and counter the enemy.
    • Utility Usage: Use your agent's abilities strategically to disrupt the enemy and support your team.

    Weapons

    Valorant offers a variety of weapons, each with its own characteristics. Here's an overview of the different types of weapons:

    Rifles

    • Vandal: High damage per shot, reliable for headshots. Ideal for mid-range combat. (Damage: 160 to the head, 40 to the body, Fire Rate: 9.25 shots/sec)
    • Phantom: Lower damage per shot compared to the Vandal but has a higher fire rate and less recoil. (Damage: 156 to the head, 39 to the body, Fire Rate: 11 shots/sec)

    SMGs

    • Stinger: High rate of fire with a smaller magazine size. Effective in close-range battles. (Damage: 65 to the head, 30 to the body, Fire Rate: 16.67 shots/sec)
    • Spectre: Low recoil and high fire rate. Suitable for close to mid-range combat. (Damage: 68 to the head, 34 to the body, Fire Rate: 13 shots/sec)

    Sniper Rifles

    • Operator: One-shot kill to the chest and head. High damage but has a slow rate of fire and movement penalty. (Damage: 202 to the head, 255 to the body, Fire Rate: 0.5 shots/sec)
    • Marshal: Lever-action sniper with high damage per shot. Ideal for long-range engagements. (Damage: 202 to the head, 101 to the body, Fire Rate: 0.5 shots/sec)

    Shotguns

    • Shorty: Extremely close-range weapon with a small magazine. Useful in tight situations. (Damage: 240 to the head, 160 to the body, Fire Rate: 3 shots/sec)
    • Judge: Fully automatic shotgun with a large spread. Effective for clearing tight spaces. (Damage: 25 per pellet to the head, 10 per pellet to the body, Fire Rate: 10 shots/sec)

    Sidearms

    • Classic: Default sidearm with basic functionality. Can be used in a pinch. (Damage: 65 to the head, 30 to the body, Fire Rate: 5 shots/sec)
    • Ghost: Silenced sidearm with higher damage and accuracy. Effective for stealthy plays. (Damage: 75 to the head, 30 to the body, Fire Rate: 5.55 shots/sec)
    • Sheriff: High damage sidearm with low magazine capacity. Powerful in skilled hands. (Damage: 202 to the head, 101 to the body, Fire Rate: 0.9 shots/sec)

    Machine Guns

    • Bulldog: A versatile rifle with a good balance between damage and fire rate. (Damage: 35 to the head, 24 to the body, Fire Rate: 8 shots/sec)
    • Ares: Fully automatic machine gun with a large magazine and high fire rate. (Damage: 35 to the head, 25 to the body, Fire Rate: 10 shots/sec)

    Others

    • Odin: Heavy machine gun with a very high fire rate and large magazine. Ideal for suppressive fire. (Damage: 50 to the head, 35 to the body, Fire Rate: 15 shots/sec)
